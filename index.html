Janarkush: The Song of the Mountains

An animated legend of harmony, courage, and the song that saved a world.

Inspired by Kyrgyz mythology.

Logline

When an ancient evil rises, twin brothers must protect a mythical bird and transform a cursed creation into a timeless instrument of harmony.

Main Characters

🌐 Türkçe Versiyon (Tilda)

  • Kambar — Father, protector, moral compass.
  • Aysal — Silent strength and maternal wisdom.
  • Aksuu & Koksuu — Twin boys, opposite energies, same courage.
  • Ka’ar — Sorcerer of destruction.
  • Kashan — Witch of laziness and false sparkle.
  • Janarkush — Mythical bird of balance, inspired by the spirit of Umai.

Antagonists

  • Ka’ar — A once-banished sorcerer who feeds on fear and despair. He seeks to destroy Janarkush and silence all harmony. Mysterious, terrifying, but not invincible.
  • Kashan — A flashy, manipulative witch — comical in appearance but dangerous in intent. She embodies Laziness and lures others into passivity and false comfort.

Ka’ar’s Servants

  • Jeztirmak — Seduction with claws.
  • Choyunbash — Strength without mind.
  • Jelmoguz — The fallen warrior.
  • Kuzkun — The watcher in the sky.

Supporting Characters

  • Janarkush — A mythical bird of light and memory. She sees through illusion and inspires truth. Her song awakens harmony, not destruction.
  • Aysal — The boys’ mother. Strong, graceful, and deeply connected to nature. Even when cursed, her wisdom continues to guide her sons.

Symbolic Guardians

  • Kambarkan Ata — Spirit of the Horsekeepers
  • Cholpon Ata — Spirit of the Shepherds
  • Dekhan Baba — Spirit of the Farmers

Visual World

The visual style of Janarkush blends poetic animation with cultural realism. It draws from Kyrgyz mountain landscapes, traditional dress, and mythological textures — creating a timeless but emotionally grounded world.

Key Locations

  • Forest Clearing — A sacred meadow surrounded by tall trees and yurts. Where the family lives and harmony is remembered.
  • Cliff of Echoes — A place of reckoning. Where truths are revealed, and heroes fall.
  • Forest of Separation — A dark pine forest. Where brothers part ways — and destiny takes root.

Why This Story Matters

In a world flooded with violence and cynicism, Janarkush tells a different tale — where courage is quiet, harmony is heroic, and healing comes through music, not weapons.

Our Cultural Voice

Janarkush is not a museum story. It’s living folklore — reshaped for today, carried by music, and rooted in the values of one of the world’s oldest cultures.

The komuz, the kalpak, the tunduk — these are not decorations. They are living codes. Symbols of Kyrgyz identity and creativity that deserve to be seen, heard, and celebrated beyond borders.

We believe that animation can carry memory. And that stories from the mountains of Central Asia can sit proudly beside legends from every corner of the world.

Our Mission

Janarkush is more than a story — it’s a cultural voice. In a global animation industry dominated by Western and East Asian stories, this project brings Central Asian mythology to the screen for the first time.

Through the music of the komuz, the image of the kalpak, and the wisdom of a mythical bird, Janarkush shares the emotional heritage of the Kyrgyz people — a story of harmony, balance, and memory.

We believe that even small cultures carry epic truths. And that animation can be a bridge, not just a spectacle.
